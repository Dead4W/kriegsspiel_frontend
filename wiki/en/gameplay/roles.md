# Gameplay by roles

This page gives a practical role-based flow for a match.

## Umpire

### Before war starts

1. Creates a room and checks key settings (map, limited ammo, time/weather modifiers, personal maps).
2. Copies team links (`umpire`, `red`, `blue`) and sends each participant only their own link.
3. Gives missions and constraints (objectives, available forces, win conditions).
4. Confirms every player spawned a general and completed initial setup.

### During war

1. Starts war and controls the timeline.
2. Processes and applies orders, keeps command queues healthy.
3. Sends reports to teams and updates information when observations are confirmed.
4. Monitors notifications: units without orders, without ammo, messengers without tasks.
5. Resolves conflicting or impossible orders and records final rulings.

### End of match

1. Ends war.
2. Summarizes outcomes: objective status, losses, key mistakes, and strong decisions.

## Team player (Red / Blue)

### Before war starts

1. Joins through the correct team link.
2. Spawns a general (required).
3. Places units according to the briefing.
4. Prepares opening orders and clarifies ambiguous conditions with the umpire.

### During war

1. Keeps orders short and testable:
   - where to move;
   - who to engage;
   - where to hold;
   - when to retreat.
2. Tracks reports and updates the plan when new information appears.
3. Keeps command queues active for critical units.
4. Uses messengers and delivery orders intentionally, not as spam.

### Example of a good order

- Weak: "Attack the right side."
- Better: "Move 2nd battalion to the southern forest edge, establish defense, open fire on confirmed enemy targets at effective range; if losses exceed 30%, fall back 300 m to reserve line."
